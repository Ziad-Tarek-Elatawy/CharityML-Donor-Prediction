# Machine Learning Models Research

This document outlines the theoretical research, real-world applications, and selection justifications for the three supervised learning models chosen for the Census Income dataset classification task.

| Model | Real-World Application | Strengths & Ideal Scenarios | Weaknesses & Poor Scenarios | Justification for Census Dataset |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Random Forest** | **Credit Card Fraud Detection:** Analyzes transaction patterns and histories to classify transactions as legitimate or fraudulent. | **Strengths:** Handles mixed data types natively, robust to outliers, and provides feature importance metrics.<br><br>**Ideal Scenario:** Tabular datasets with complex, non-linear relationships requiring minimal hyperparameter tuning. | **Weaknesses:** Memory-intensive, can be slow to predict if the forest is deep, and acts as a "black box" lacking single-tree transparency.<br><br>**Poor Scenario:** Real-time applications requiring ultra-low latency or highly sparse data. | Census datasets are classically tabular with a mix of discrete (categorical) and continuous variables. Random Forest is a strong candidate because it handles this mixed data natively and captures complex demographic interactions without requiring massive feature engineering. |
| **AdaBoost** | **Customer Churn Prediction:** Evaluates usage data and demographic profiles to identify which customers are most likely to cancel subscriptions. | **Strengths:** Highly accurate, builds sequential models to correct previous mistakes, and is often resistant to overfitting on clean data.<br><br>**Ideal Scenario:** Binary classification on tabular data where maximizing predictive accuracy is the primary goal. | **Weaknesses:** Extremely sensitive to noisy data and outliers, as it will over-focus on unrepresentative anomalies. Sequential training cannot be parallelized.<br><br>**Poor Scenario:** Datasets with a high volume of outliers or mislabeled targets. | The boundaries between income classes in demographic data can be subtle. AdaBoost's mechanism of assigning higher weights to difficult-to-classify individuals ensures the final model pays close attention to the nuances of the population, maximizing overall accuracy. |
| **Support Vector Machines (SVM)** | **Text Classification / Spam Filtering:** Draws precise decision boundaries based on word frequencies to separate spam from legitimate emails. | **Strengths:** Exceptionally effective in high-dimensional spaces. The "kernel trick" allows it to find complex, non-linear decision boundaries.<br><br>**Ideal Scenario:** Complex datasets where the margin of separation is distinct and the feature space is highly dimensional. | **Weaknesses:** Training time scales cubically ($O(n^3)$), making it painfully slow for massive datasets. Highly sensitive to feature scales.<br><br>**Poor Scenario:** Datasets with >100,000 rows or highly overlapping classes where a clear margin is absent. | To use an SVM on census data, categorical variables must be one-hot encoded, which radically expands the feature space. SVMs are uniquely equipped to handle this resulting high dimensionality. Using an RBF kernel, it can project the data to find a clear hyperplane separating income groups. |
